Calxo's calculators can be embedded on any website, blog, internal HR portal or LMS — for free. Bloggers, finance creators, advisors and HR teams use the embed to add a working calculator next to their own content without rebuilding the math themselves.

## How embedding works

1. Open any [calculator](/) on Calxo
2. Click **Share, embed or cite** below the article
3. Copy the iframe snippet from the **Embed** tab
4. Paste it into your page (WordPress, Webflow, Ghost, Squarespace, plain HTML — anywhere HTML is allowed)

The iframe auto-resizes to fit the calculator widget, so you don't need to set a height. The embed includes a small "Powered by Calxo" footer with a link back to the source page — that's the only attribution we ask for.

## What you get in the embed

Each embedded calculator is the **full interactive widget** — same formula, same INR formatting, same defaults as the calxo.in version. No watermarks, no ads, no popups. Mobile-responsive out of the box. Loads in under 50KB plus the Plus Jakarta Sans webfont.

The widget renders entirely on the user's browser — no inputs are sent to any server.

## Where you can embed

Anywhere that accepts HTML or an iframe block:

- **WordPress** (HTML block, or Custom HTML widget)
- **Webflow** (Embed component)
- **Ghost** (HTML card)
- **Squarespace** (Code block)
- **Wix** (HTML iframe)
- **Notion** (Embed block — paste the embed URL directly)
- **Substack** (HTML in Markdown)
- **Internal HR portals** running on Confluence, SharePoint, Salesforce community
- **LMS platforms** like Moodle and Canvas

For platforms that don't allow scripts (e.g. Notion), the auto-resize won't work — the iframe will use the default 520px height. For full-height iframes, paste the snippet on a platform that supports script execution.

## Attribution requirements

The embed automatically includes a "Powered by Calxo — Free Indian finance calculators" link in the iframe footer. By embedding, you agree to keep this attribution intact. That's the only ask.

You're free to:

- Use Calxo embeds in commercial blogs, portals, or services
- Wrap the iframe in your own UI (add a heading, intro paragraph, etc.)
- Use multiple Calxo calculators on the same page
- Embed in client deliverables (financial planning reports, HR onboarding materials, etc.)

You can't:

- Strip the "Powered by Calxo" footer or its link
- Re-host the calculator JavaScript on your own server (use the iframe; it's self-contained)
- Imply that the calculator is your own work

## Citing Calxo

For Quora answers, blog posts, research notes or guides where you reference a Calxo calculator without embedding, use the **Cite** tab in the share modal. We provide three formats:

- **Text** — for plain-text contexts (academic citations, chat messages)
- **HTML** — when your platform renders links (most blogs, Quora, Reddit, Stack Exchange)
- **BibTeX** — for academic papers, theses, technical documents

The citation includes the article URL, author (Vignesh), reviewer (Prem Anand) and access date.
