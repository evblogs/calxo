Sameer took a ₹3.3 lakh field sales executive role in Ahmedabad. The offer letter said ₹27,500 a month. The bank credit was ₹23,339. The whole gap is your own EPF, the ₹2,400 professional tax, and the employer PF plus gratuity that live inside CTC and never reach you. In-hand at 3.3 LPA works out to about 85% of CTC.

## What ₹3.3 lakh CTC actually contains

Standard 50% basic structure at ₹3.3L:

| Component | Annual | Monthly |
|---|---|---|
| Basic salary | ₹1,65,000 | ₹13,750 |
| HRA (50% of basic) | ₹82,500 | ₹6,875 |
| Special allowance | ₹54,764 | ₹4,564 |
| Employer PF (12% of basic) | ₹19,800 | ₹1,650 |
| Gratuity provision (4.81%) | ₹7,936 | ₹661 |
| **Total CTC** | **₹3,30,000** | **₹27,500** |

Employer PF plus gratuity comes to ₹27,736. That money sits inside the ₹3.3 lakh CTC and never lands in your salary account. Your gross salary, the part payroll actually pays, is ₹3,02,264 a year.

## 3.3 LPA in-hand salary: the take-home math

| Item | Annual | Monthly |
|---|---|---|
| Gross salary (excl. employer PF + gratuity) | ₹3,02,264 | ₹25,189 |
| Less: Employee PF | ₹19,800 | ₹1,650 |
| Less: Professional tax (Karnataka) | ₹2,400 | ₹200 |
| Less: Income tax | ₹0 | ₹0 |
| **In-hand** | **₹2,80,064** | **₹23,339** |

Taxable income after the ₹75,000 standard deduction is ₹2,27,264, and the 87A rebate wipes out any tax the slabs produce. So income tax is zero. The only money leaving your gross is your own PF of ₹19,800 and ₹2,400 of professional tax.

## Why in-hand is about 85% of CTC here

Your in-hand at ₹3.3 lakh is ₹2,80,064, roughly 85% of CTC. Early on almost everything you earn is yours, which is exactly when a savings habit is cheapest to start. The share reaching your hand only drops from here: about 80% near ₹16 lakh, 75% near ₹30 lakh, under 60% at ₹1 crore.

The ₹1,650 a month into PF is part of that gap. Your employer adds the same again, so close to ₹3,300 a month is quietly compounding for you. Track it with the [EPF calculator](/salary/epf-calculator/).
