Priya signed a ₹4.2 lakh business-analyst trainee role in Gurgaon. The offer letter said ₹35,000 a month. The bank credit was ₹29,758. None of that gap is tax. It is your own EPF, a ₹2,400 professional tax, and the employer PF plus gratuity that sit inside CTC. In-hand at 4.2 LPA works out to about 85% of CTC.

## What ₹4.2 lakh CTC actually contains

Standard 50% basic structure at ₹4.2L:

| Component | Annual | Monthly |
|---|---|---|
| Basic salary | ₹2,10,000 | ₹17,500 |
| HRA (50% of basic) | ₹1,05,000 | ₹8,750 |
| Special allowance | ₹69,699 | ₹5,808 |
| Employer PF (12% of basic) | ₹25,200 | ₹2,100 |
| Gratuity provision (4.81%) | ₹10,101 | ₹842 |
| **Total CTC** | **₹4,20,000** | **₹35,000** |

Employer PF plus gratuity comes to ₹35,301. That money sits inside the ₹4.2 lakh CTC and never lands in your salary account. Your gross salary, the part payroll actually pays, is ₹3,84,699 a year.

## 4.2 LPA in-hand salary: the take-home math

| Item | Annual | Monthly |
|---|---|---|
| Gross salary (excl. employer PF + gratuity) | ₹3,84,699 | ₹32,058 |
| Less: Employee PF | ₹25,200 | ₹2,100 |
| Less: Professional tax (Karnataka) | ₹2,400 | ₹200 |
| Less: Income tax | ₹0 | ₹0 |
| **In-hand** | **₹3,57,099** | **₹29,758** |

Taxable income after the ₹75,000 standard deduction is ₹3,09,699, and the 87A rebate wipes out any tax the slabs produce. So income tax is zero. The only money leaving your gross is your own PF of ₹25,200 and ₹2,400 of professional tax.

## Why in-hand is about 85% of CTC here

Your in-hand at ₹4.2 lakh is ₹3,57,099, roughly 85% of CTC. This is the widest your take-home ratio ever gets. It only shrinks as tax bites at higher salaries. The share reaching your hand only drops from here: about 80% near ₹16 lakh, 75% near ₹30 lakh, under 60% at ₹1 crore.

The ₹2,100 a month into PF is part of that gap. Your employer adds the same again, so close to ₹4,200 a month is quietly compounding for you. Track it with the [EPF calculator](/salary/epf-calculator/).
