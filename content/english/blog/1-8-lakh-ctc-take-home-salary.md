Deepak took a ₹1.8 lakh data-entry operator role in Nagpur. The offer letter said ₹15,000 a month. The bank credit was ₹12,639. The whole gap is your own EPF, the ₹2,400 professional tax, and the employer PF plus gratuity that live inside CTC and never reach you. In-hand at 1.8 LPA works out to about 84% of CTC.

## What ₹1.8 lakh CTC actually contains

Standard 50% basic structure at ₹1.8L:

| Component | Annual | Monthly |
|---|---|---|
| Basic salary | ₹90,000 | ₹7,500 |
| HRA (50% of basic) | ₹45,000 | ₹3,750 |
| Special allowance | ₹29,871 | ₹2,489 |
| Employer PF (12% of basic) | ₹10,800 | ₹900 |
| Gratuity provision (4.81%) | ₹4,329 | ₹361 |
| **Total CTC** | **₹1,80,000** | **₹15,000** |

Employer PF plus gratuity comes to ₹15,129. That money sits inside the ₹1.8 lakh CTC and never lands in your salary account. Your gross salary, the part payroll actually pays, is ₹1,64,871 a year.

## 1.8 LPA in-hand salary: the take-home math

| Item | Annual | Monthly |
|---|---|---|
| Gross salary (excl. employer PF + gratuity) | ₹1,64,871 | ₹13,739 |
| Less: Employee PF | ₹10,800 | ₹900 |
| Less: Professional tax (Karnataka) | ₹2,400 | ₹200 |
| Less: Income tax | ₹0 | ₹0 |
| **In-hand** | **₹1,51,671** | **₹12,639** |

Taxable income after the ₹75,000 standard deduction is ₹89,871, and the 87A rebate wipes out any tax the slabs produce. So income tax is zero. The only money leaving your gross is your own PF of ₹10,800 and ₹2,400 of professional tax.

## Why in-hand is about 84% of CTC here

Your in-hand at ₹1.8 lakh is ₹1,51,671, roughly 84% of CTC. Early on almost everything you earn is yours, which is exactly when a savings habit is cheapest to start. The share reaching your hand only drops from here: about 80% near ₹16 lakh, 75% near ₹30 lakh, under 60% at ₹1 crore.

The ₹900 a month into PF is part of that gap. Your employer adds the same again, so close to ₹1,800 a month is quietly compounding for you. Track it with the [EPF calculator](/salary/epf-calculator/).
