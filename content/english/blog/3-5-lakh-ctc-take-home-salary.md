Rohit took a ₹3.5 lakh support engineer role in Bhopal. The offer letter said ₹29,167 a month. The bank credit was ₹24,765. There is no income tax at this level, so every rupee missing is EPF, ₹2,400 of professional tax, or the employer PF and gratuity buried in CTC. In-hand at 3.5 LPA works out to about 85% of CTC.

## What ₹3.5 lakh CTC actually contains

Standard 50% basic structure at ₹3.5L:

| Component | Annual | Monthly |
|---|---|---|
| Basic salary | ₹1,75,000 | ₹14,583 |
| HRA (50% of basic) | ₹87,500 | ₹7,292 |
| Special allowance | ₹58,082 | ₹4,840 |
| Employer PF (12% of basic) | ₹21,000 | ₹1,750 |
| Gratuity provision (4.81%) | ₹8,418 | ₹701 |
| **Total CTC** | **₹3,50,000** | **₹29,167** |

Employer PF plus gratuity comes to ₹29,418. That money sits inside the ₹3.5 lakh CTC and never lands in your salary account. Your gross salary, the part payroll actually pays, is ₹3,20,582 a year.

## 3.5 LPA in-hand salary: the take-home math

| Item | Annual | Monthly |
|---|---|---|
| Gross salary (excl. employer PF + gratuity) | ₹3,20,582 | ₹26,715 |
| Less: Employee PF | ₹21,000 | ₹1,750 |
| Less: Professional tax (Karnataka) | ₹2,400 | ₹200 |
| Less: Income tax | ₹0 | ₹0 |
| **In-hand** | **₹2,97,182** | **₹24,765** |

Taxable income after the ₹75,000 standard deduction is ₹2,45,582, and the 87A rebate wipes out any tax the slabs produce. So income tax is zero. The only money leaving your gross is your own PF of ₹21,000 and ₹2,400 of professional tax.

## Why in-hand is about 85% of CTC here

Your in-hand at ₹3.5 lakh is ₹2,97,182, roughly 85% of CTC. At this level tax is zero, so the ratio is near its ceiling. It falls steadily as CTC climbs. The share reaching your hand only drops from here: about 80% near ₹16 lakh, 75% near ₹30 lakh, under 60% at ₹1 crore.

The ₹1,750 a month into PF is part of that gap. Your employer adds the same again, so close to ₹3,500 a month is quietly compounding for you. Track it with the [EPF calculator](/salary/epf-calculator/).
