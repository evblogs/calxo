Pooja took a ₹2.8 lakh content writer role in Indore. The offer letter said ₹23,333 a month. The bank credit was ₹19,772. None of that gap is tax. It is your own EPF, a ₹2,400 professional tax, and the employer PF plus gratuity that sit inside CTC. In-hand at 2.8 LPA works out to about 85% of CTC.

## What ₹2.8 lakh CTC actually contains

Standard 50% basic structure at ₹2.8L:

| Component | Annual | Monthly |
|---|---|---|
| Basic salary | ₹1,40,000 | ₹11,667 |
| HRA (50% of basic) | ₹70,000 | ₹5,833 |
| Special allowance | ₹46,466 | ₹3,872 |
| Employer PF (12% of basic) | ₹16,800 | ₹1,400 |
| Gratuity provision (4.81%) | ₹6,734 | ₹561 |
| **Total CTC** | **₹2,80,000** | **₹23,333** |

Employer PF plus gratuity comes to ₹23,534. That money sits inside the ₹2.8 lakh CTC and never lands in your salary account. Your gross salary, the part payroll actually pays, is ₹2,56,466 a year.

## 2.8 LPA in-hand salary: the take-home math

| Item | Annual | Monthly |
|---|---|---|
| Gross salary (excl. employer PF + gratuity) | ₹2,56,466 | ₹21,372 |
| Less: Employee PF | ₹16,800 | ₹1,400 |
| Less: Professional tax (Karnataka) | ₹2,400 | ₹200 |
| Less: Income tax | ₹0 | ₹0 |
| **In-hand** | **₹2,37,266** | **₹19,772** |

Taxable income after the ₹75,000 standard deduction is ₹1,81,466, and the 87A rebate wipes out any tax the slabs produce. So income tax is zero. The only money leaving your gross is your own PF of ₹16,800 and ₹2,400 of professional tax.

## Why in-hand is about 85% of CTC here

Your in-hand at ₹2.8 lakh is ₹2,37,266, roughly 85% of CTC. This is the widest your take-home ratio ever gets. It only shrinks as tax bites at higher salaries. The share reaching your hand only drops from here: about 80% near ₹16 lakh, 75% near ₹30 lakh, under 60% at ₹1 crore.

The ₹1,400 a month into PF is part of that gap. Your employer adds the same again, so close to ₹2,800 a month is quietly compounding for you. Track it with the [EPF calculator](/salary/epf-calculator/).
