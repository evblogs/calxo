Faisal moved to a ₹8.5 lakh role as a marketing associate in Kochi and had to pick a tax regime on the joining form. New regime: ₹60,430 a month, zero income tax. Old regime with no real deductions: ₹55,385 a month, because ₹8.5 lakh gets taxed ₹60,540 there. Same CTC, ₹5,045 a month difference, decided by one checkbox. At ₹8.5 lakh the new regime wins by a distance.

## What ₹8.5 lakh CTC actually contains

Standard 50% basic structure at ₹8.5L:

| Component | Annual | Monthly |
|---|---|---|
| Basic salary | ₹4,25,000 | ₹35,417 |
| HRA (50% of basic) | ₹2,12,500 | ₹17,708 |
| Special allowance | ₹1,41,058 | ₹11,755 |
| Employer PF (12% of basic) | ₹51,000 | ₹4,250 |
| Gratuity provision (4.81%) | ₹20,442 | ₹1,704 |
| **Total CTC** | **₹8,50,000** | **₹70,833** |

Employer PF plus gratuity comes to ₹71,442. That money sits inside the ₹8.5 lakh CTC and never lands in your salary account. Your gross salary, the part payroll actually pays, is ₹7,78,558 a year.

## Take-home calculation (new regime)

| Item | Annual | Monthly |
|---|---|---|
| Gross salary (excl. employer PF + gratuity) | ₹7,78,558 | ₹64,880 |
| Less: Employee PF | ₹51,000 | ₹4,250 |
| Less: Professional tax (Karnataka) | ₹2,400 | ₹200 |
| Less: Income tax | ₹0 | ₹0 |
| **In-hand** | **₹7,25,158** | **₹60,430** |

Tax working: gross ₹7,78,558 minus the ₹75,000 standard deduction leaves taxable income of ₹7,03,558. The slab tax adds up to ₹15,178 (₹15,178 in the 5% slab), but taxable income stays under the ₹12 lakh line, so the section 87A rebate of up to ₹60,000 wipes the whole amount. Tax payable: zero.

## New regime vs old regime at this salary

This is the band where the regime choice is the whole game. Under the new regime, taxable income of ₹7,03,558 sits under the ₹12 lakh line, so the section 87A rebate still reaches it and income tax is zero. Switch to the old regime with no major deductions and the same ₹8.5 lakh is taxed ₹60,540 a year, pulling monthly in-hand down to ₹55,385. That is ₹60,540 a year, gone for ticking the wrong box.

The old regime only catches up if you genuinely stack deductions: the full ₹1.5 lakh under 80C, ₹25,000 of 80D health cover, ₹50,000 of NPS under 80CCD(1B), and real HRA exemption from rent paid. Without most of that, stay on the new regime. Run your actual numbers both ways in the [take-home calculator](/salary/takehome-calculator/) before you commit on the joining form.
