## Real Estate Prices in Major Indian Cities

In India's real estate market, metro cities like Mumbai, Delhi, and Bengaluru have seen property prices skyrocket over the past decade. Mumbai, for instance, averages ₹15,000 per sq ft, while Bengaluru is somewhat more affordable at ₹8,000 per sq ft. This dramatic variation prompts many to debate whether it's more economical to buy or rent. For some, owning a home is a dream, but this dream often comes with a hefty price tag. Others wonder if renting might be a smarter financial move given the high prices. 

On the flip side, renting in these cities isn't exactly cheap. A 2BHK apartment in a decent Mumbai locality can set you back ₹50,000 per month. Bengaluru offers a relative bargain, at about ₹30,000 monthly. These figures should account for a sizeable chunk of your monthly income. So, what should you do? Kya karna chahiye? Let’s break it down using a buy vs rent calculator India offers, and see where your money is best spent.

## Cost of Borrowing Money

Interest rates on home loans in India usually hover around 8-9% per annum. These are generally long-term commitments, often stretching over 20 years. If you're considering taking a home loan for ₹50 lakh, at an 8% interest rate, your monthly EMI could easily be around ₹41,822. This impacts your financial freedom considerably, leaving less room for savings or investments.

On a different note, renting offers flexibility and lower initial costs. You might pay only a deposit that equates to a few months’ rent, unlike the substantial down payment needed when buying a property. Priya, a software engineer in Pune, recently chose to rent an apartment. For her, the rent of ₹25,000 a month on a salary of ₹15 lakh per annum allowed for better cash flow. This scenario illustrates how you can have a lifestyle suited to your needs without a debilitating loan EMI.

## Opportunity Cost and Investment Gains

Don't ignore opportunity cost. Keep it top of mind. Funds committed to a property purchase could be invested elsewhere. This cash could fuel your mutual funds and SIP purchases instead. If you're shelling out ₹1 crore for a home today, that’s ₹1 crore unavailable for investment in markets where even modest returns can build wealth over time.

A thumb rule often used is the 5% rule. If the yearly rent is less than 5% of the property value, then renting may be the better option. For example, renting a property valued at ₹1 crore should cost you less than ₹5 lakh yearly to make it financially sensible. Use this rule in your buy vs rent calculator India style, and get a clearer picture.

## Inflation and Property Appreciation

Property generally appreciates over time, offering potential long-term gains. However, there's no guarantee. Some regions appreciate faster than others. Over the last ten years, housing prices in Indian metros have appreciated at average rates of 5-6% annually. While this can provide significant equity in the future, it's not without risk. 

At the same time, rental rates typically rise due to inflation, often around 4-5% per year. Renting can become more burdensome on your finances as time passes and rates rise. However, renters are also spared from some costs homeowners face, like property taxes, maintenance fees, and insurance.
