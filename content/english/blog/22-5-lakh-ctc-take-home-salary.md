Rahul negotiated a ₹22.5 lakh package as a senior engineer in Kolkata, up from a lower band, and expected the raise to land in full. It did not. Monthly in-hand is ₹1,43,202, which is about 76% of CTC. The 87A rebate is gone at this level, so income tax of ₹2,05,065 a year is now a real line on the payslip, not a number the rebate quietly erases.

## What ₹22.5 lakh CTC actually contains

Standard 50% basic structure at ₹22.5L:

| Component | Annual | Monthly |
|---|---|---|
| Basic salary | ₹11,25,000 | ₹93,750 |
| HRA (50% of basic) | ₹5,62,500 | ₹46,875 |
| Special allowance | ₹3,73,388 | ₹31,116 |
| Employer PF (12% of basic) | ₹1,35,000 | ₹11,250 |
| Gratuity provision (4.81%) | ₹54,112 | ₹4,509 |
| **Total CTC** | **₹22,50,000** | **₹1,87,500** |

Employer PF plus gratuity comes to ₹1,89,112. That money sits inside the ₹22.5 lakh CTC and never lands in your salary account. Your gross salary, the part payroll actually pays, is ₹20,60,888 a year.

## Take-home calculation (new regime)

| Item | Annual | Monthly |
|---|---|---|
| Gross salary (excl. employer PF + gratuity) | ₹20,60,888 | ₹1,71,741 |
| Less: Employee PF | ₹1,35,000 | ₹11,250 |
| Less: Professional tax (Karnataka) | ₹2,400 | ₹200 |
| Less: Income tax | ₹2,05,065 | ₹17,089 |
| **In-hand** | **₹17,18,423** | **₹1,43,202** |

Tax working: gross ₹20,60,888 minus the ₹75,000 standard deduction leaves taxable income of ₹19,85,888. Slab tax is ₹20,000 in the 5% slab, ₹40,000 in the 10% slab, ₹60,000 in the 15% slab, ₹77,178 in the 20% slab, totalling ₹1,97,178. Add 4% cess of ₹7,887 and the income tax is ₹2,05,065 a year. The 87A rebate does not apply once taxable income is past ₹12 lakh.

## Where the 87A rebate stops and tax begins

At ₹22.5 lakh the 87A rebate is no longer in the picture. Taxable income of ₹19,85,888 is past the ₹12 lakh ceiling, so the full slab tax of ₹1,97,178 is payable, which after 4% cess is ₹2,05,065 a year. Every extra rupee of CTC at this level is taxed at your top slab, so a raise here delivers a lot less to your hand than the headline suggests.

The single best move in this band is employer NPS under 80CCD(2). Up to 14% of basic routed as an employer NPS contribution is deductible even in the new regime, which at ₹22.5 lakh can shave ₹20,000 to ₹35,000 off your annual tax. Ask HR whether your flexi-benefits portal allows it. Confirm the new figure in the [income tax calculator](/tax/income-tax-calculator/).
