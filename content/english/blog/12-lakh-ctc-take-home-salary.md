Then that first SMS from HDFC: salary credited, but only ₹85,395. Riya called me in full panic. “Bhai, where did all my money go? Offer letter mein kuch aur likha tha!” Classic mix-up. HR tells you one thing, payroll delivers another. Every Indian salary journey starts with this confusion. And trust me, it’s not just freshers. Even people with 10 years’ experience struggle to explain their payslip. If this sounds familiar, you’re in the right place. Let’s break down exactly how a ₹12 lakh CTC becomes a very different take-home.

## 1. The five layers between offer letter and bank account

There’s a whole obstacle course between your offer letter and your actual bank credit. Five layers, to be precise. First, there’s the split between what you actually get and what’s set aside for you. Employer PF and gratuity, both compulsory, are promised but not paid out every month. Then there’s the statutory deductions — PF again, but from your side this time, and professional tax if your state applies (hello, Karnataka). Next, income tax. Even if the new regime gives you a breather at ₹12 lakh, other years or other regimes might not be so kind. Layer four: fancy allowances in your CTC, like LTA or variable pay, that often never reach your account in full. Last, stuff that’s just accounting magic — the so-called “CTC” padding with things you’ll never actually see as rupees. Riya learned it the hard way. Don’t be like Riya, at least not after reading this.

## 2. What CTC actually contains

CTC is always marketed as your “total annual package,” but half of it isn’t even paid to you directly. Start with the basics: ₹6,00,000 basic salary, exactly 50% of the ₹12 lakh CTC. Your HRA is ₹2,40,000, set at 40% of basic since Pune isn’t a metro. Special allowance is ₹2,59,830 — the leftovers after all the “must-have” items are filled in. Then, employer PF: 12% of basic, so ₹72,000. Gratuity provision, which is 4.81% of basic, adds another ₹28,860. Both employer PF and gratuity are included in your CTC but you don’t get them as monthly cash. That’s a trick HR loves. So, even before any taxes or deductions, your take-home is already lower than the CTC headline. It’s not a scam, but it isn’t full disclosure either.

Sometimes numbers speak louder than paragraphs. Here’s exactly how a ₹12,00,000 CTC breaks down for FY 2025-26, using the most common break-up:

Professional tax is small, just ₹200 per month in Karnataka, but that’s your chai-pani fund gone. Most surprisingly, income tax is zero at this level for FY 2025-26, if you use the new regime. That’s not a typo.

## 4. How the regime choice changes take-home

At ₹12 lakh CTC, people think tax planning matters a lot. Actually, this is the one slab where the new regime is so generous, you pay zero income tax after standard deduction and Section 87A rebate. Your taxable income is ₹10,24,140, and the full tax liability is wiped out by the ₹60,000 rebate. Old regime, though? Not so lucky. Here’s a quick comparison:

| Regime             | Taxable Income (₹) | Income Tax (₹) | Take-home (₹) | Monthly Take-home (₹) |
|--------------------|-------------------|----------------|---------------|-----------------------|
| **New Regime**     | 10,24,140         | 0              | 10,24,740     | 85,395                |
| **Old Regime**     | 9,24,140*         | 28,600         | 9,96,140      | 83,012                |

\*Assuming 80C (PF) deduction, ₹50,000 standard deduction, no HRA exemption (since rent may or may not be claimed).

Most people on this slab should just pick new regime and sleep well. Don’t sweat about tax-saving mutual funds, bhai, your take-home doesn’t change unless you cross that ₹12 lakh edge. Want to dig deeper? See our [old vs new tax regime article](/blog/old-vs-new-tax-regime-fy-2025-26/).

## 5. Five things HR won't tell you

First: Variable pay is often “up to” a certain number, not guaranteed. If you get 80% payout instead of 100%, your actual take-home drops. Second: EPF interest is tax-free, but the employer PF part is only yours if you stay long enough, and there are withdrawal rules. Third: Gratuity isn’t real money unless you complete five years — quit early, and that ₹28,860 per annum vanishes. Fourth: Food coupons, NPS, and insurance allowances can be added to your CTC, but unless you opt-in, they’re just window dressing. Fifth: Professional tax is state-specific. Riya didn’t know about the ₹200 per month deduction in Pune until her payslip arrived. Don’t expect HR to spell all this out during the offer negotiation. The details are always hidden in the fine print or in your [take-home salary calculator](/salary/takehome-calculator/).

## 6. How to actually increase take-home

This is what everyone wants to know. Most hacks don’t work at this slab, but some do. Employer NPS: If your company offers it, opt for employer NPS up to 10% of basic. It’s over and above 80C, so lowers your taxable income directly (but new regime? Not so helpful). Food coupons: Salary restructuring for meal cards means less taxable income, and you actually use them for lunch. Medical and LTA: If your company allows, claim these by submitting bills. Flexi-benefit plans: Some IT companies offer you a “flexi basket” to allocate towards tax-free heads, like phone bills, books, or even children’s education. Don’t ignore this — most people just let it default to “special allowance,” which is fully taxable. There’s no jugaad to bypass PF or professional tax. Don’t. Seriously. You’ll regret it at withdrawal time.

## 8. FAQ section

### Tax is zero, so why isn’t my take-home higher?

Because CTC isn’t just salary. Employer PF and gratuity, about ₹1 lakh, are not paid out monthly. Employee PF is deducted too. Only gross salary minus statutory deductions lands in your bank.

### Can I get more HRA exemption if I live in Mumbai?

HRA calculation depends on whether your city is metro (50% of basic) or non-metro (40%). Mumbai, Delhi, Kolkata, Chennai are metros. In Pune, HRA is 40% of basic. Use our [HRA exemption calculator](/salary/hra-calculator/) if you move.

### What if I switch to old regime and claim all deductions?

You can, but at ₹12 lakh CTC, standard deduction and 80C (PF) rarely make old regime better. New regime is cleaner. Only if you have massive Section 80D, 80E, or HRA claims does old regime start to help. Check exact numbers with our [income tax calculator](/tax/income-tax-calculator/).

### Is PF withdrawal taxable later?

PF is tax-free if withdrawn after five years of service. Early withdrawal? Interest portion is taxable. Don’t break your EPF just to boost take-home, unless you really need it.

### What about EMI eligibility at this salary?

Banks use your net take-home, not CTC, for loan eligibility. At ₹85,395 per month, you can get a home loan EMI up to ₹42,000–₹51,000 depending on FOIR. Use our [EMI calculator](/loan/emi-calculator/) for specifics.
