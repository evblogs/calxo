Ritika moved to a ₹9.6 lakh role as a business analyst in Nagpur and had to pick a tax regime on the joining form. New regime: ₹68,276 a month, zero income tax. Old regime with no real deductions: ₹61,485 a month, because ₹9.6 lakh gets taxed ₹81,497 there. Same CTC, ₹6,791 a month difference, decided by one checkbox. At ₹9.6 lakh the new regime wins by a distance.

## What ₹9.6 lakh CTC actually contains

Standard 50% basic structure at ₹9.6L:

| Component | Annual | Monthly |
|---|---|---|
| Basic salary | ₹4,80,000 | ₹40,000 |
| HRA (50% of basic) | ₹2,40,000 | ₹20,000 |
| Special allowance | ₹1,59,312 | ₹13,276 |
| Employer PF (12% of basic) | ₹57,600 | ₹4,800 |
| Gratuity provision (4.81%) | ₹23,088 | ₹1,924 |
| **Total CTC** | **₹9,60,000** | **₹80,000** |

Employer PF plus gratuity comes to ₹80,688. That money sits inside the ₹9.6 lakh CTC and never lands in your salary account. Your gross salary, the part payroll actually pays, is ₹8,79,312 a year.

## Take-home calculation (new regime)

| Item | Annual | Monthly |
|---|---|---|
| Gross salary (excl. employer PF + gratuity) | ₹8,79,312 | ₹73,276 |
| Less: Employee PF | ₹57,600 | ₹4,800 |
| Less: Professional tax (Karnataka) | ₹2,400 | ₹200 |
| Less: Income tax | ₹0 | ₹0 |
| **In-hand** | **₹8,19,312** | **₹68,276** |

Tax working: gross ₹8,79,312 minus the ₹75,000 standard deduction leaves taxable income of ₹8,04,312. The slab tax adds up to ₹20,431 (₹20,000 in the 5% slab, ₹431 in the 10% slab), but taxable income stays under the ₹12 lakh line, so the section 87A rebate of up to ₹60,000 wipes the whole amount. Tax payable: zero.

## New regime vs old regime at this salary

This is the band where the regime choice is the whole game. Under the new regime, taxable income of ₹8,04,312 sits under the ₹12 lakh line, so the section 87A rebate still reaches it and income tax is zero. Switch to the old regime with no major deductions and the same ₹9.6 lakh is taxed ₹81,497 a year, pulling monthly in-hand down to ₹61,485. That is ₹81,497 a year, gone for ticking the wrong box.

The old regime only catches up if you genuinely stack deductions: the full ₹1.5 lakh under 80C, ₹25,000 of 80D health cover, ₹50,000 of NPS under 80CCD(1B), and real HRA exemption from rent paid. Without most of that, stay on the new regime. Run your actual numbers both ways in the [take-home calculator](/salary/takehome-calculator/) before you commit on the joining form.
