Anjali accepted a ₹3.6 lakh marketing associate role in Surat. The offer letter said ₹30,000 a month. The bank credit was ₹25,478. That gap is not tax at all. It is your EPF, ₹2,400 professional tax, and the employer PF and gratuity that never leave the CTC line. In-hand at 3.6 LPA works out to about 85% of CTC.

## What ₹3.6 lakh CTC actually contains

Standard 50% basic structure at ₹3.6L:

| Component | Annual | Monthly |
|---|---|---|
| Basic salary | ₹1,80,000 | ₹15,000 |
| HRA (50% of basic) | ₹90,000 | ₹7,500 |
| Special allowance | ₹59,742 | ₹4,978 |
| Employer PF (12% of basic) | ₹21,600 | ₹1,800 |
| Gratuity provision (4.81%) | ₹8,658 | ₹722 |
| **Total CTC** | **₹3,60,000** | **₹30,000** |

Employer PF plus gratuity comes to ₹30,258. That money sits inside the ₹3.6 lakh CTC and never lands in your salary account. Your gross salary, the part payroll actually pays, is ₹3,29,742 a year.

## 3.6 LPA in-hand salary: the take-home math

| Item | Annual | Monthly |
|---|---|---|
| Gross salary (excl. employer PF + gratuity) | ₹3,29,742 | ₹27,478 |
| Less: Employee PF | ₹21,600 | ₹1,800 |
| Less: Professional tax (Karnataka) | ₹2,400 | ₹200 |
| Less: Income tax | ₹0 | ₹0 |
| **In-hand** | **₹3,05,742** | **₹25,478** |

Taxable income after the ₹75,000 standard deduction is ₹2,54,742, and the 87A rebate wipes out any tax the slabs produce. So income tax is zero. The only money leaving your gross is your own PF of ₹21,600 and ₹2,400 of professional tax.

## Why in-hand is about 85% of CTC here

Your in-hand at ₹3.6 lakh is ₹3,05,742, roughly 85% of CTC. Hold on to this ratio. It is about as high as in-hand ever runs before income tax starts eating in. The share reaching your hand only drops from here: about 80% near ₹16 lakh, 75% near ₹30 lakh, under 60% at ₹1 crore.

The ₹1,800 a month into PF is part of that gap. Your employer adds the same again, so close to ₹3,600 a month is quietly compounding for you. Track it with the [EPF calculator](/salary/epf-calculator/).
