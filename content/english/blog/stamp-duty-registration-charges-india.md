## Understanding Stamp Duty and Registration

Buying property in India involves multiple layers of cost, of which stamp duty and registration charges are an integral part. These charges, often overlooked initially, can significantly impact your budget as you proceed with a property transaction. Stamp duty is a tax levied on property transactions by the government, while registration charges are fees for the formal registration of the property documents. Both are mandatory. The amount you pay varies by state. Let's go through this in detail.

## State-Specific Rates for 2026

Every state and union territory in India determines its own stamp duty rates. In Maharashtra, for example, the stamp duty on property is 6% of the transaction value in urban areas as of 2026. Meanwhile, in Karnataka, it's around 5%. Someone buying a ₹50 lakh flat in Pune must account for a ₹3 lakh stamp duty. These differences can greatly affect how much you'll end up spending. The disparity across regions can make it quite a puzzle, but a smart investor calculates meticulously.

To understand how these charges apply in other regions, check this table for reference:

| State          | Stamp Duty Rate | Registration Charges |
|----------------|-----------------|----------------------|
| Maharashtra    | 6%              | 1%                   |
| Karnataka      | 5%              | 1%                   |
| Delhi          | 4% (5% for males) | 1%                |
| Tamil Nadu     | 7%              | 1%                   |
| West Bengal    | 7%              | 1%                   |
