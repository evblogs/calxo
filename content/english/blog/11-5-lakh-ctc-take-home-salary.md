Pooja moved to a ₹11.5 lakh role as a marketing associate in Bhubaneswar and had to pick a tax regime on the joining form. New regime: ₹81,829 a month, zero income tax. Old regime with no real deductions: ₹71,992 a month, because ₹11.5 lakh gets taxed ₹1,18,043 there. Same CTC, ₹9,837 a month difference, decided by one checkbox. At ₹11.5 lakh the new regime wins by a distance.

## What ₹11.5 lakh CTC actually contains

Standard 50% basic structure at ₹11.5L:

| Component | Annual | Monthly |
|---|---|---|
| Basic salary | ₹5,75,000 | ₹47,917 |
| HRA (50% of basic) | ₹2,87,500 | ₹23,958 |
| Special allowance | ₹1,90,842 | ₹15,904 |
| Employer PF (12% of basic) | ₹69,000 | ₹5,750 |
| Gratuity provision (4.81%) | ₹27,658 | ₹2,305 |
| **Total CTC** | **₹11,50,000** | **₹95,833** |

Employer PF plus gratuity comes to ₹96,658. That money sits inside the ₹11.5 lakh CTC and never lands in your salary account. Your gross salary, the part payroll actually pays, is ₹10,53,342 a year.

## Take-home calculation (new regime)

| Item | Annual | Monthly |
|---|---|---|
| Gross salary (excl. employer PF + gratuity) | ₹10,53,342 | ₹87,779 |
| Less: Employee PF | ₹69,000 | ₹5,750 |
| Less: Professional tax (Karnataka) | ₹2,400 | ₹200 |
| Less: Income tax | ₹0 | ₹0 |
| **In-hand** | **₹9,81,942** | **₹81,829** |

Tax working: gross ₹10,53,342 minus the ₹75,000 standard deduction leaves taxable income of ₹9,78,342. The slab tax adds up to ₹37,834 (₹20,000 in the 5% slab, ₹17,834 in the 10% slab), but taxable income stays under the ₹12 lakh line, so the section 87A rebate of up to ₹60,000 wipes the whole amount. Tax payable: zero.

## New regime vs old regime at this salary

This is the band where the regime choice is the whole game. Under the new regime, taxable income of ₹9,78,342 sits under the ₹12 lakh line, so the section 87A rebate still reaches it and income tax is zero. Switch to the old regime with no major deductions and the same ₹11.5 lakh is taxed ₹1,18,043 a year, pulling monthly in-hand down to ₹71,992. That is ₹1,18,043 a year, gone for ticking the wrong box.

The old regime only catches up if you genuinely stack deductions: the full ₹1.5 lakh under 80C, ₹25,000 of 80D health cover, ₹50,000 of NPS under 80CCD(1B), and real HRA exemption from rent paid. Without most of that, stay on the new regime. Run your actual numbers both ways in the [take-home calculator](/salary/takehome-calculator/) before you commit on the joining form.
