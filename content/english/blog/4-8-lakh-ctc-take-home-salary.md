Farhan took a ₹4.8 lakh backend developer role in Mumbai. The offer letter said ₹40,000 a month. The bank credit was ₹34,038. None of that gap is tax. It is your own EPF, a ₹2,400 professional tax, and the employer PF plus gratuity that sit inside CTC. In-hand at 4.8 LPA works out to about 85% of CTC.

## What ₹4.8 lakh CTC actually contains

Standard 50% basic structure at ₹4.8L:

| Component | Annual | Monthly |
|---|---|---|
| Basic salary | ₹2,40,000 | ₹20,000 |
| HRA (50% of basic) | ₹1,20,000 | ₹10,000 |
| Special allowance | ₹79,656 | ₹6,638 |
| Employer PF (12% of basic) | ₹28,800 | ₹2,400 |
| Gratuity provision (4.81%) | ₹11,544 | ₹962 |
| **Total CTC** | **₹4,80,000** | **₹40,000** |

Employer PF plus gratuity comes to ₹40,344. That money sits inside the ₹4.8 lakh CTC and never lands in your salary account. Your gross salary, the part payroll actually pays, is ₹4,39,656 a year.

## 4.8 LPA in-hand salary: the take-home math

| Item | Annual | Monthly |
|---|---|---|
| Gross salary (excl. employer PF + gratuity) | ₹4,39,656 | ₹36,638 |
| Less: Employee PF | ₹28,800 | ₹2,400 |
| Less: Professional tax (Karnataka) | ₹2,400 | ₹200 |
| Less: Income tax | ₹0 | ₹0 |
| **In-hand** | **₹4,08,456** | **₹34,038** |

Taxable income after the ₹75,000 standard deduction is ₹3,64,656, and the 87A rebate wipes out any tax the slabs produce. So income tax is zero. The only money leaving your gross is your own PF of ₹28,800 and ₹2,400 of professional tax.

## Why in-hand is about 85% of CTC here

Your in-hand at ₹4.8 lakh is ₹4,08,456, roughly 85% of CTC. This is the widest your take-home ratio ever gets. It only shrinks as tax bites at higher salaries. The share reaching your hand only drops from here: about 80% near ₹16 lakh, 75% near ₹30 lakh, under 60% at ₹1 crore.

The ₹2,400 a month into PF is part of that gap. Your employer adds the same again, so close to ₹4,800 a month is quietly compounding for you. Track it with the [EPF calculator](/salary/epf-calculator/).
