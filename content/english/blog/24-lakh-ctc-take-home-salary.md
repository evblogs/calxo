Deepa negotiated a ₹24 lakh package as a product manager in Mumbai, up from a lower band, and expected the raise to land in full. It did not. Monthly in-hand is ₹1,50,986, which is about 75% of CTC. The 87A rebate is gone at this level, so income tax of ₹2,40,053 a year is now a real line on the payslip, not a number the rebate quietly erases.

## What ₹24 lakh CTC actually contains

Standard 50% basic structure at ₹24L:

| Component | Annual | Monthly |
|---|---|---|
| Basic salary | ₹12,00,000 | ₹1,00,000 |
| HRA (50% of basic) | ₹6,00,000 | ₹50,000 |
| Special allowance | ₹3,98,280 | ₹33,190 |
| Employer PF (12% of basic) | ₹1,44,000 | ₹12,000 |
| Gratuity provision (4.81%) | ₹57,720 | ₹4,810 |
| **Total CTC** | **₹24,00,000** | **₹2,00,000** |

Employer PF plus gratuity comes to ₹2,01,720. That money sits inside the ₹24 lakh CTC and never lands in your salary account. Your gross salary, the part payroll actually pays, is ₹21,98,280 a year.

## Take-home calculation (new regime)

| Item | Annual | Monthly |
|---|---|---|
| Gross salary (excl. employer PF + gratuity) | ₹21,98,280 | ₹1,83,190 |
| Less: Employee PF | ₹1,44,000 | ₹12,000 |
| Less: Professional tax (Karnataka) | ₹2,400 | ₹200 |
| Less: Income tax | ₹2,40,053 | ₹20,004 |
| **In-hand** | **₹18,11,827** | **₹1,50,986** |

Tax working: gross ₹21,98,280 minus the ₹75,000 standard deduction leaves taxable income of ₹21,23,280. Slab tax is ₹20,000 in the 5% slab, ₹40,000 in the 10% slab, ₹60,000 in the 15% slab, ₹80,000 in the 20% slab, ₹30,820 in the 25% slab, totalling ₹2,30,820. Add 4% cess of ₹9,233 and the income tax is ₹2,40,053 a year. The 87A rebate does not apply once taxable income is past ₹12 lakh.

## Where the 87A rebate stops and tax begins

At ₹24 lakh the 87A rebate is no longer in the picture. Taxable income of ₹21,23,280 is past the ₹12 lakh ceiling, so the full slab tax of ₹2,30,820 is payable, which after 4% cess is ₹2,40,053 a year. Every extra rupee of CTC at this level is taxed at your top slab, so a raise here delivers a lot less to your hand than the headline suggests.

The single best move in this band is employer NPS under 80CCD(2). Up to 14% of basic routed as an employer NPS contribution is deductible even in the new regime, which at ₹24 lakh can shave ₹20,000 to ₹35,000 off your annual tax. Ask HR whether your flexi-benefits portal allows it. Confirm the new figure in the [income tax calculator](/tax/income-tax-calculator/).
