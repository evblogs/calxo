Ravi joined on a ₹2.4 lakh BPO associate role in Coimbatore. The offer letter said ₹20,000 a month. The bank credit was ₹16,919. There is no income tax at this level, so every rupee missing is EPF, ₹2,400 of professional tax, or the employer PF and gratuity buried in CTC. In-hand at 2.4 LPA works out to about 85% of CTC.

## What ₹2.4 lakh CTC actually contains

Standard 50% basic structure at ₹2.4L:

| Component | Annual | Monthly |
|---|---|---|
| Basic salary | ₹1,20,000 | ₹10,000 |
| HRA (50% of basic) | ₹60,000 | ₹5,000 |
| Special allowance | ₹39,828 | ₹3,319 |
| Employer PF (12% of basic) | ₹14,400 | ₹1,200 |
| Gratuity provision (4.81%) | ₹5,772 | ₹481 |
| **Total CTC** | **₹2,40,000** | **₹20,000** |

Employer PF plus gratuity comes to ₹20,172. That money sits inside the ₹2.4 lakh CTC and never lands in your salary account. Your gross salary, the part payroll actually pays, is ₹2,19,828 a year.

## 2.4 LPA in-hand salary: the take-home math

| Item | Annual | Monthly |
|---|---|---|
| Gross salary (excl. employer PF + gratuity) | ₹2,19,828 | ₹18,319 |
| Less: Employee PF | ₹14,400 | ₹1,200 |
| Less: Professional tax (Karnataka) | ₹2,400 | ₹200 |
| Less: Income tax | ₹0 | ₹0 |
| **In-hand** | **₹2,03,028** | **₹16,919** |

Taxable income after the ₹75,000 standard deduction is ₹1,44,828, and the 87A rebate wipes out any tax the slabs produce. So income tax is zero. The only money leaving your gross is your own PF of ₹14,400 and ₹2,400 of professional tax.

## Why in-hand is about 85% of CTC here

Your in-hand at ₹2.4 lakh is ₹2,03,028, roughly 85% of CTC. At this level tax is zero, so the ratio is near its ceiling. It falls steadily as CTC climbs. The share reaching your hand only drops from here: about 80% near ₹16 lakh, 75% near ₹30 lakh, under 60% at ₹1 crore.

The ₹1,200 a month into PF is part of that gap. Your employer adds the same again, so close to ₹2,400 a month is quietly compounding for you. Track it with the [EPF calculator](/salary/epf-calculator/).
