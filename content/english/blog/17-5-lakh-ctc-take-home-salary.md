Manish negotiated a ₹17.5 lakh package as a product manager in Lucknow, up from a lower band, and expected the raise to land in full. It did not. Monthly in-hand is ₹1,15,163, which is about 79% of CTC. The 87A rebate is gone at this level, so income tax of ₹1,13,554 a year is now a real line on the payslip, not a number the rebate quietly erases.

## What ₹17.5 lakh CTC actually contains

Standard 50% basic structure at ₹17.5L:

| Component | Annual | Monthly |
|---|---|---|
| Basic salary | ₹8,75,000 | ₹72,917 |
| HRA (50% of basic) | ₹4,37,500 | ₹36,458 |
| Special allowance | ₹2,90,412 | ₹24,201 |
| Employer PF (12% of basic) | ₹1,05,000 | ₹8,750 |
| Gratuity provision (4.81%) | ₹42,088 | ₹3,507 |
| **Total CTC** | **₹17,50,000** | **₹1,45,833** |

Employer PF plus gratuity comes to ₹1,47,088. That money sits inside the ₹17.5 lakh CTC and never lands in your salary account. Your gross salary, the part payroll actually pays, is ₹16,02,912 a year.

## Take-home calculation (new regime)

| Item | Annual | Monthly |
|---|---|---|
| Gross salary (excl. employer PF + gratuity) | ₹16,02,912 | ₹1,33,576 |
| Less: Employee PF | ₹1,05,000 | ₹8,750 |
| Less: Professional tax (Karnataka) | ₹2,400 | ₹200 |
| Less: Income tax | ₹1,13,554 | ₹9,463 |
| **In-hand** | **₹13,81,958** | **₹1,15,163** |

Tax working: gross ₹16,02,912 minus the ₹75,000 standard deduction leaves taxable income of ₹15,27,912. Slab tax is ₹20,000 in the 5% slab, ₹40,000 in the 10% slab, ₹49,187 in the 15% slab, totalling ₹1,09,187. Add 4% cess of ₹4,367 and the income tax is ₹1,13,554 a year. The 87A rebate does not apply once taxable income is past ₹12 lakh.

## Where the 87A rebate stops and tax begins

At ₹17.5 lakh the 87A rebate is no longer in the picture. Taxable income of ₹15,27,912 is past the ₹12 lakh ceiling, so the full slab tax of ₹1,09,187 is payable, which after 4% cess is ₹1,13,554 a year. Every extra rupee of CTC at this level is taxed at your top slab, so a raise here delivers a lot less to your hand than the headline suggests.

The single best move in this band is employer NPS under 80CCD(2). Up to 14% of basic routed as an employer NPS contribution is deductible even in the new regime, which at ₹17.5 lakh can shave ₹20,000 to ₹35,000 off your annual tax. Ask HR whether your flexi-benefits portal allows it. Confirm the new figure in the [income tax calculator](/tax/income-tax-calculator/).
