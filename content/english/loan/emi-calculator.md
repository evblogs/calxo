## How EMI is calculated

Your monthly EMI (Equated Monthly Instalment) is calculated using a fixed mathematical formula:

`EMI = P × r × (1 + r)ⁿ / [(1 + r)ⁿ − 1]`

Where:

- **P** is the principal loan amount (the money you borrow)
- **r** is the monthly interest rate, calculated as `(annual rate ÷ 12) ÷ 100`
- **n** is the total number of monthly instalments (loan tenure in years × 12)

This formula is the **reducing-balance method**, which is what every Indian bank — SBI, HDFC, ICICI, Axis, Bajaj Finserv, Tata Capital — uses for home loans, personal loans, and most retail credit. Flat-rate EMIs are rare today and almost always more expensive than they look — avoid them.

## Worked example — ₹25 lakh home loan at 8.5% for 20 years

Suppose you take a home loan of **₹25,00,000** at an annual interest rate of **8.5%** for a tenure of **20 years**.

Plug into the formula:

- P = 25,00,000
- r = 8.5 ÷ 12 ÷ 100 = **0.007083** (monthly rate)
- n = 20 × 12 = **240** months

Apply the EMI formula and you get:

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Monthly EMI | **₹21,696** |
| Total interest paid over 20 years | **₹27,07,049** |
| Total amount paid (principal + interest) | **₹52,07,049** |

Notice that **you pay more in interest (₹27 lakh) than the original loan (₹25 lakh)** when the tenure is long. That's why shortening the tenure or making part-prepayments matter so much in home loans.

## How interest rates affect your EMI

Even a 1% change in the interest rate can shift your total interest by ₹3–5 lakh on a ₹25 lakh, 20-year home loan:

| Annual rate | Monthly EMI | Total interest |
|---|---|---|
| 7.50% | ₹20,135 | ₹23,32,478 |
| 8.00% | ₹20,911 | ₹25,18,649 |
| 8.50% | ₹21,696 | ₹27,07,049 |
| 9.00% | ₹22,493 | ₹28,98,360 |
| 9.50% | ₹23,300 | ₹30,92,067 |

If banks are quoting 9.5% and you negotiate down to 8.5%, you save **₹3.85 lakh** over the loan tenure. This is why comparing 3–4 lender quotes before signing matters more than the freebies a bank throws in.

## How tenure affects your EMI

Stretching the tenure lowers your monthly EMI but increases total interest dramatically. Same ₹25 lakh loan at 8.5%:

| Tenure | Monthly EMI | Total interest |
|---|---|---|
| 10 years | ₹30,997 | ₹12,19,646 |
| 15 years | ₹24,617 | ₹19,31,019 |
| 20 years | ₹21,696 | ₹27,07,049 |
| 25 years | ₹20,123 | ₹35,36,840 |
| 30 years | ₹19,219 | ₹44,18,805 |

A 30-year tenure looks attractive (only ₹19,219 a month) but you end up paying **₹19 lakh more in interest** versus 20 years. Take the shortest tenure you can comfortably afford, and use part-prepayments to shorten further.

## Frequently asked questions

### Can I prepay my loan EMI to reduce interest?

Yes. RBI rules say that **floating-rate home loans cannot charge prepayment penalties**. For fixed-rate or non-housing loans, banks may charge 1–4% on the prepaid amount. Even one extra EMI per year can shave 4–5 years off a 20-year home loan and save several lakh in interest.

### Is EMI fixed or does it change with the RBI repo rate?

If your loan is on a **floating rate** (most home loans), the EMI typically stays the same but the **tenure changes** when the RBI changes its repo rate. Some banks will instead change the EMI itself — check your loan agreement. Fixed-rate loans don't change at all.

### Does the EMI calculator include processing fees and GST?

No. This calculator gives you the pure EMI based on principal, rate, and tenure. Processing fees (typically 0.5–1% of the loan amount), 18% GST on those fees, stamp duty, and insurance are charged separately and don't affect the EMI itself.

### What's the difference between flat-rate and reducing-balance EMI?

**Reducing-balance** (what this calculator uses) charges interest only on the outstanding balance — so as you pay down the principal, the interest portion of each EMI shrinks. **Flat-rate** charges interest on the full original amount for the entire tenure, which makes the effective rate roughly double the quoted rate. Always confirm which method is being used.

### Can I use this for personal, car, or education loans?

Yes. The EMI formula is identical for all reducing-balance loans. Just plug in your loan amount, the bank's annual rate, and the tenure. As a rough guide:

- **Personal loans:** 10.5%–24% rates, 1–5 year tenures
- **Car loans:** 8.5%–12%, 3–7 years
- **Education loans:** 8.5%–13%, 5–15 years
- **Business loans:** 11%–22%, 1–7 years

### Why does my bank's EMI quote differ slightly from this calculator?

Tiny differences (₹50–₹500) usually come from rounding rules, processing fees being added to the principal, or the bank using simple interest for a partial first month. The numbers from this calculator match standard reducing-balance math. If your bank's quote is significantly higher, ask them to break down where the difference is coming from.
